Who Owns Tubi? Tubi Company History

Tubi, Inc. is an American over-the-top content platform and ad-supported streaming service that has gained popularity among viewers seeking free TV and movie streaming options. In this article, we will delve into the ownership details of Tubi, exploring its parent company, acquisition history, and ownership structure.

Founded on April 1, 2014, in San Francisco, California, Tubi was the brainchild of Farhad Massoudi and Thomas Ahn Hicks. Since its inception, the company has experienced remarkable growth, attracting millions of monthly active users and expanding its presence across multiple countries, such as Australia, Canada, Mexico, and India.

In March 2020, Tubi caught the attention of Fox Corporation, a prominent media and entertainment conglomerate. Fox Corporation recognized the potential of Tubi’s ad-supported streaming service and acquired the company for $440 million, making it their subsidiary.

Despite the acquisition, Tubi maintains its independence within Fox Corporation. Farhad Massoudi, the founder of Tubi, continues to serve as the CEO, overseeing the company’s operations and future growth.

Tubi’s mission is to provide a diverse range of free, high-quality entertainment to its users, with an extensive content library spanning over 40,000 films and television series. The service features content from major studios like The Walt Disney Company, NBCUniversal, Warner Bros. Discovery, and Paramount Global.

Tubi’s Growth and User Base

Since its launch in 2014, Tubi has experienced remarkable growth in its user base. The platform has become a popular choice among audiences looking for free TV and movie streaming services.

In January 2021, Tubi reached 33 million monthly active users, demonstrating its appeal and potential. The following year, in January 2023, Tubi’s user base grew to an impressive 64 million monthly active users, indicating its continued success and expanding reach.

As of September 2023, Tubi has further solidified its position in the streaming industry, surpassing 74 million monthly active users. This substantial user base showcases Tubi’s ability to attract and retain a large audience, making it one of the most watched free streaming platforms in the United States.

The continuous growth of Tubi’s user base is a testament to the platform’s compelling content offerings and user-friendly experience. By providing a diverse range of movies and TV shows, Tubi has successfully captured the attention of millions of viewers, establishing itself as a leading player in the streaming market.

Tubi’s Advertising and Revenue Model

Tubi operates as an ad-supported streaming service, relying on advertising revenue to support its free content offerings. The platform provides a unique revenue model that benefits both advertisers and viewers.

Through Tubi’s ad-supported streaming model, advertisers have the opportunity to deliver video ads to a large and engaged audience across various platforms. Tubi uses a real-time bidding platform, enabling advertisers to bid on ad placements in real-time, ensuring efficient ad delivery and optimal targeting.

For viewers, Tubi’s ad-supported model allows them to access a wide range of free movies and TV shows without the need for a subscription or pay-per-view fees. By watching ads, viewers support the service and enjoy quality content without any additional cost.

Benefits of Tubi’s ad-supported streaming:

  • Free access to a diverse library of movies and TV shows
  • No subscription or pay-per-view fees
  • Highly targeted and relevant video ads
  • Real-time bidding for efficient ad delivery
  • Supports the growth and availability of free streaming content

Tubi’s revenue model has proven to be successful, generating significant advertising revenue and contributing to the overall financial performance of Fox Corporation. In the company’s quarterly earnings reports, Tubi has been highlighted as a key revenue driver.
