DescriptionThis highly skilled, powerhouse Trainer prominent in Kanto is the grandson of the famous Professor Oak. Red is both his lifelong rival and his best friend.

Blue (グリーン Green) is a Pokémon Trainer in Pokémon Masters EX, based on the character of the same name in the core series. He forms a sync pair with Pidgeot, which is capable of Mega Evolving. This sync pair was first made available on September 3, 2019. The pair's potential can be upgraded to 6★ EX from November 11, 2020.

Blue in his sygna suit forms a sync pair with Blastoise, which is capable of Mega Evolving. This sync pair was first made available on August 28, 2020. This pair can be raised to 6★ EX from the day it was released.

Blue in his Classic clothes forms a sync pair with Aerodactyl, which is capable of Mega Evolving. This sync pair was first made available on September 18, 2021. This pair can be raised to 6★ EX from the day it was released.

Blue also forms a sync pair with his Arcanine. This sync pair was first made available on February 17, 2022. The pair's potential can be upgraded to 6★ EX from August 21, 2022.

Blue also forms a sync pair with his Exeggutor. This sync pair can be obtained by reaching Friendship Level 100 with Blue in the Trainer Lodge. This pair could be raised to 6★ EX beginning on March 29, 2023, though this pair lacks an EX style.

Blue in his Neo Champion attire forms a sync pair with his Zapdos. This pair can be obtained through a limited Master Fair sync pair scout beginning on September 1, 2023. This pair could be raised to 6★ EX since it first released.

Blue in his Classic clothes also forms a sync pair with his Charizard, which is capable of Dynamaxing. This pair was first made available through a Mix sync pair scout beginning on May 1, 2024. This pair can be raised to 6★ EX since it first released, though this pair lacks an EX style.

History

Blue has experienced similar events to his core series counterpart. Blue received his first partner Pokémon, Squirtle, from his grandfather Professor Oak .[4] He left Pallet Town at the same time as Red and Leaf and formed a rivalry with each of them.[5] While on his journey, Blue caught a Growlithe and revived an Aerodactyl from some amber.[6][7] His Growlithe eventually evolved into Arcanine, and his Squirtle eventually evolved into Blastoise. At some point, Blue obtained a Pidgeot, which he uses to fly to different towns,[8] an Exeggutor, and a Charizard. After collecting Gym Badges, Blue defeated the Indigo League Elite Four and became the Champion of Kanto. However, he lost this title when Red defeated him shortly after, earning him the nickname "the phantom Champion".[9]

After losing to Red, Blue caught a Zapdos in the hopes of getting ahead of both Red and Leaf, but he later learned that they both already owned one of the legendary birds as well.[10]

Pidgeot

Raising this sync pair to 6★ EX will unlock Song Key 01002, which allows "Battle! (Blue) (Pokémon Masters EX Version)" to be played in the Jukebox.

Trivia

  • Blue's rival is Red, as mentioned in his character description, he also has the first partner Pokémon with a type advantage over Red's, and Red was the one who defeated him when he was Champion.
  • Blue & Pidgeot are one of four sync pairs who have had an increased recruitment chance in a seasonal scout without being a seasonal sync pair. The other three are Karen & Houndoom, Hilda & Tepig, and Brendan & Treecko.
  • Blue is the only Trainer whose seven Pokémon were all introduced in the same generation as the Trainer.
  • Blue is the only Trainer to form two sync pairs while wearing the same outfit to have a distinct EX style for both pairs.
  • Sygna Suit Blue & Blastoise were the first fair-exclusive Water-type sync pair.
  • Sygna Suit Blue & Blastoise were the last support sync pair to have a sync move that has an innate power-increasing effect.
  • Sygna Suit Blue's debut banner was originally scheduled to run until September 28, 2020. However, on September 16, 2020, it was announced that more tiles would be added to his sync grid and the duration of his scout would be extended along with this adjustment. The scout duration was extended immediately, but the additions to his sync grid did not go into effect until players updated to version 2.1.0 at the end of September.[15]
  • All versions of Blue use special voice-over lines upon entering a team that contains certain Trainers (regardless of their partner Pokémon). Those Trainers include Red, Leaf, Elaine, Chase, Professor Oak, and Raihan.
    • Blue also uses special voice-over lines upon winning a battle with Red, Leaf, Elaine, Chase, or Raihan in the same team.
  • Blue and Red are the first characters to be both a Champion and a Neo Champion.
  • In his Champion outfit's sync pair story, Blue reveals that he caught his Zapdos while he was training in Kanto after losing to Red, in hopes of getting ahead of both Red and Leaf. Afterward, he learned that they both already owned one of the legendary birds as well.
  • Sygna Suit Blue & Blastoise were the first support sync pair to receive both horizontal and vertical sync grid expansions.
  • Blue is the first Trainer to form seven different sync pairs.

Origin

Blue's design is based on his appearance in Pokémon HeartGold and SoulSilver. Pidgeot is one of the Pokémon that Blue uses in the core series games—being his lead Pokémon in many battles and his highest-level Pokémon in HeartGold and SoulSilver, with it capable of Mega Evolving in Pokémon Sun, Moon, Ultra Sun, and Ultra Moon. In addition, Blue's Pokémon Origins and Pokémon Adventures counterparts have been depicted with a Pidgeot. Pidgeot's Dex entry comes from Pokémon Ruby and Sapphire.

Arcanine is a Pokémon that Blue uses in the Kanto-based core series games if the player chooses Charmander or Squirtle as their first partner Pokémon, as well in other core series games he's appeared in. In addition, Blue's anime and Pokémon Adventures counterparts have been depicted with an Arcanine. Arcanine's Dex entry comes from Pokémon Ultra Sun.

Exeggutor is a Pokémon that Blue uses in the Kanto-based core series games if the player chooses Charmander or Bulbasaur as their first partner Pokémon, as well in other core series games he's appeared in. In addition, Blue's Pokémon Origins and Pokémon Adventures counterparts have been depicted with an Exeggutor. Exeggutor's Dex entry comes from Pokémon Ultra Sun.

Blastoise is the final evolution of Squirtle, one of the first partner Pokémon of Kanto in the core series games. Blastoise is Blue's first partner Pokémon if the player chooses Charmander as their first partner Pokémon in the Kanto-based core series games. In addition, Blue's anime, and Pokémon Zensho counterparts have been depicted with Blastoise. Blastoise's Dex entry comes from Pokémon Ruby and Sapphire.

Blue's classic design is based on his appearance in Pokémon FireRed and LeafGreen. Aerodactyl is a Pokémon that Blue has used in the core series games since Pokémon Black 2 and White 2, with it being capable of Mega Evolving in Pokémon Sun, Moon, Ultra Sun, and Ultra Moon. Aerodactyl's Dex entry comes from Pokémon Ultra Sun.

Charizard is the final evolution of Charmander, one of the first partner Pokémon of Kanto in the core series games. Charizard is Blue's first partner Pokémon if the player chooses Bulbasaur as their first partner Pokémon in the Kanto-based core series games. Blue also uses a Charizard as his signature Pokémon in Pokémon: Let's Go, Pikachu! and Let's Go, Eevee!. In addition, Blue's Pokémon Adventures counterpart has been depicted with a Charizard. Charizard's Dex entry comes from Pokémon Ruby and Sapphire.

Blue's Pokémon Adventures counterpart temporarily uses Green's Zapdos during the manga. Zapdos's Dex entry comes from Pokémon Ruby and Sapphire.
